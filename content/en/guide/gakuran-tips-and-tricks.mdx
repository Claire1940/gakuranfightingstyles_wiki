<Section icon={Target} title="Combat Mechanics and Advanced Mastery">

Success in Gakuran combat relies on more than just clicking faster than your opponent. You must master the art of the "Perfect Block" and understand the clashing system. When two fighters strike at the same time, a grapple or "clash" occurs. The fighter who initiated the swing first typically wins the encounter, shoving the opponent back and opening them up for a follow-up combo.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Master the Parry">
    Hold the block key (F) just as the opponent's strike is about to land. A successful parry stuns the enemy for approximately one second, allowing for a full M1 combo.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Back-Hit Strategy">
    Do not attack an opponent head-on if they are blocking. Use your dash to circle around to their back. Hits landed on the back cannot be parried or blocked effectively.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Range Verification">
    Visit the Dojo on the second floor. Use the practice dummies to determine exactly where your "hit range" ends. Knowing your maximum reach allows you to "kite" shorter players.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Guard Pressure">
Keep an eye on the "Guard PS" of your chosen style. Even if an opponent blocks every hit, high Guard PS styles like Slugger will eventually deplete their health through the block, forcing them to take risks.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Defensive Maneuvers and Survival">

In high-level Gakuran play, your defense is just as important as your offense. Simply holding block is not enough, as experienced players will dash behind you or use high-chip damage styles to break your guard. You must incorporate "Evasive Dodging" into your movement patterns.

**Survival Tactics:**
- **Auto-Parry**: If you are being hit by a fast combo, hold F immediately. While not a guaranteed escape, it increases the chance of catching a perfect block window.
- **Circle Dashing**: Always move in a circular motion around your opponent. This makes it harder for them to track your hitbox and easier for you to find an opening in their back.
- **Dojo Training**: The second floor Dojo isn't just for dummies; it's the best place to spar with friends to learn the animations of different styles without the risk of losing rank.

<Callout type="warning" title="Stamina Management">
