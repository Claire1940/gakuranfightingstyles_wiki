<Section icon={Shield} title="Combat Mastery and Techniques">

To succeed in Gakuran, you must move beyond simple button mashing. The combat system rewards timing, spacing, and psychological warfare. Understanding the "Parry-Combo" cycle is essential for surviving encounters with veteran players.

**The Art of the Parry**
Parrying is performed by pressing **F** just before an opponent's strike connects. A successful parry leaves the attacker vulnerable for a brief window, allowing you to start your own combo. Do not hold the block button indefinitely, as your guard can be broken by heavy attacks or specific combos.

**Advanced Combo Strings**
A common mistake among new players is spamming the M1 (Left Click) button. This makes your attacks predictable and easy to parry. Instead, use the following techniques:
- **The 1-2-Critical**: Hit M1 twice, then immediately follow up with a Critical attack. This often catches players who are waiting to parry the third M1 hit.
- **Dashing (Q)**: Use the Q key to dash backward or sideways. This is vital for resetting your position if your parry fails.
- **Spacing**: Keep a slight distance between yourself and the opponent. Only close the gap when you are ready to initiate a strike.

| Technique | Input | Strategic Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **M1 Spam** | Left Click | Only use against guard-broken or stunned enemies |
| **Critical** | Variant | Used to break guards and finish combos |
| **Dash** | Q Key | Essential for dodging and closing distance |
| **Sprint Attack** | Shift + M1 | Good for initiating a fight from a distance |

<Callout type="warning" title="Anti-Parry Strategy">
If you notice your opponent is parrying everything, stop your combo after the first hit and dash away. This resets the "rhythm" of the fight and prevents them from getting a free counter-attack on you.
</Callout>

</Section>
