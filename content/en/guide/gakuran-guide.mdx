<Section icon={Shield} title="Countering Legendary Styles: Wrestling, Kensei, and Capoeira">

Wrestling is widely feared due to its devastating critical attack, which grants the user hyper armor during the startup animation. This makes it impossible to cancel their attack with standard M1 strikes. Experienced Wrestlers will often play passively, baiting you into chasing them. When you attempt an M1 strike, they will activate their crit, which effectively pulls your extended hitbox directly into their slam. To counter this, you must maintain absolute awareness of their crit cooldown and your own dodge availability. If you do get slammed, you enter a blurred state for 3 to 4 seconds. During this window, do not attempt to dash, as your character will trip and fall. Do not hold block blindly, as they will easily break your guard. Your only viable option is to land a precise defensive parry to mitigate incoming damage until your vision clears.

Kensei represents a different kind of threat. Unlike Wrestling, its crit does not feature hyper armor, meaning you can cancel it with a well-timed M1 strike. However, the Kensei crit has an incredibly dangerous perk: once it lands, it completely prevents you from parrying for 2 seconds. If you try to parry during this lock-out period, you will take full damage. The correct defensive response is to immediately raise your guard to block. You must block exactly two M1 strikes from the Kensei player, and then immediately dash backward to reset the neutral game. Playing against Kensei requires a passive, range-focused approach. Use poking attacks to snipe them from a distance, punishing their attempts to close the gap.

Capoeira relies heavily on mobility and deceptive animations. The style grants a passive 25% reduction to dash cooldowns, allowing Capoeira users to zip around the arena both offensively and defensively. Their M1 animations are notoriously eccentric, which can make parrying difficult for inexperienced players. Fortunately, their crit is far less threatening than Wrestling or Kensei. The key to defeating Capoeira is patience. Expect them to dash frequently, and focus on learning their unique visual cues to land consistent parries.

<Section icon={Target} title="Handling Boxing and Common Styles">

Boxing is a high-pressure style that excels in close-quarters combat. The most dangerous elements of Boxing are its incredibly fast first M1 strike and its high-impact crit. The first M1 hitbox actually activates slightly before the visual animation plays, making it exceptionally difficult to react to on pure sight. To deal with the Boxing crit, you have a few options depending on your current posture. If your posture bar is completely full, you can safely block both hits of the crit. If the first hit of the crit connects, do not panic; immediately attempt to parry the second hit, which is relatively slow and highly readable. Ideally, you should dash away from the crit entirely to maintain a safe distance. Since most Boxing users prefer short character builds for speed, you should abuse your range and play a disciplined keep-away game.

Both Karate and Muay Thai feature ragdoll critical attacks that can deal massive damage depending on how your character falls. Unlike Wrestling, these crits do not have hyper armor and can be easily canceled with aggressive M1 pressure. Karate users benefit from a passive perk that constantly regenerates their posture, allowing them to block significantly more attacks than other styles. To crack a Karate user's defense, you must maintain relentless pressure or bait them into missing their crit, then punish their recovery frames. Muay Thai users rely on raw damage output, so outranging their attacks is your best path to victory.

Hikari and Slugger are currently the weakest styles in the Gakuran meta. Both styles suffer from extremely slow M1 strikes and highly telegraphed critical attacks. Any player with decent reaction times can easily parry every single attack they throw. If you face a Hikari or Slugger user, simply watch their startup animations, wait for the slow swing, and execute a perfect parry to open them up for a devastating counter-combo.

<Section icon={Map} title="Step-by-Step Defense Execution">

Executing proper defensive sequences under pressure is what separates novice fighters from arena masters. When fighting high-tier styles like Kensei, panicking after getting hit by a crit will guarantee a rapid defeat. Follow this precise defensive protocol to survive the Kensei parry-lock mechanic and reset the fight to neutral.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify the Kensei Crit">
    Watch for the distinct visual startup of the Kensei critical attack. If you fail to dodge or cancel it, prepare for the incoming parry-lock debuff.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Hold Block Immediately">
    Once the crit connects, do not attempt to parry. Raise your guard immediately to absorb the oncoming M1 strikes without taking direct damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Count Two M1 Blocks">
    Listen to the sound cues and watch your posture bar. Safely block exactly two consecutive light attacks from the opponent to prevent a guard break.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Dash Away to Reset">
    Immediately after the second block, execute a backward dash to escape their pressure and let your parry-lock debuff expire safely.
  </Step>
</Steps>
