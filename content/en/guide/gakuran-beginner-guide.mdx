<Section icon={Target} title="Combat Mechanics and Combo Techniques">

Fighting in Gakuran is more than just clicking. To succeed, you must master the art of the **Parry (F)** and movement. The combat system rewards patience and timing over button mashing. If you spam your M1 (Left Click) attacks, you become incredibly easy to parry, leaving you vulnerable to a devastating counter-attack.

**Offensive Strategy: The 1-2-Critical Combo**
A common mistake beginners make is finishing their full M1 string. Instead, try the "1-2-Critical" method. Hit your opponent twice with standard M1s, then immediately use your Critical attack. This often catches opponents who are waiting to parry the third M1, resulting in a guard break and a free combo opportunity.

**Defensive Strategy: Parrying and Dashing**
- **Parrying (F)**: Press F just before an enemy's strike lands. A successful parry stuns the opponent.
- **Dashing (Q)**: Use the Q key to close the gap or retreat. Dashing is essential for resetting your positioning when your stamina is low.
- **Spacing**: Always maintain a slight distance between your M1s. This "staggered" attacking makes your rhythm unpredictable.

| Mechanic | Keybind | Function | Pro Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **M1 Attack** | Left Click | Basic Strike | Don't spam; use spacing |
| **Parry** | F | Block/Stun | Watch the enemy's arm movement |
| **Critical** | R / Click | Guard Break | Use after two M1s |
| **Dash** | Q | Mobility | Use to dodge critical attacks |
| **Combat Mode** | T | Toggle Stance | Must be ON to deal damage |

<Callout type="warning" title="Stamina Management">
Every action, from dashing to attacking, consumes stamina. If your stamina bar hits zero, you cannot block or dash, making you an easy target. Always keep an eye on the blue bar.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Daily Activities and Hidden Features">

While Gakuran is primarily a fighting game, it features several interactive elements that add depth to the school life RP. Between fights, you can engage in various activities to pass the time or socialize with other students.

**The Phone System (Alt Key)**
By pressing **Alt**, you pull out your in-game smartphone. This is a "game within a game" that features a functioning UI. You can call friends, manage contacts, and even play mini-games. The phone is a central hub for your social interactions and is essential for coordinating with your group or gang.

**Music and Sports**
- **Piano/Guitar**: Located in the music room, these instruments are fully playable. The piano features a rhythm-game mechanic similar to Friday Night Funkin' (using X, C, M keys).
- **Basketball**: There is a court outside where you can shoot hoops using the F key to aim and release. (Note: This feature may occasionally be bugged during map revamps).
