<Section icon={Target} title="Advanced Combat Mechanics and Combo Mastery">

Mastering the **Gakuran complete guide** to combat requires more than just clicking your mouse. The game utilizes a sophisticated parry and guard system that punishes "spamming." If you simply mash M1, a skilled opponent will easily time a parry (F key) and leave you wide open for a devastating counter-attack.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Enable Combat Mode">
    Press the **T key** to enter your fighting stance. You will see your character's posture change, signaling that you are ready for action.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master the Parry">
    Wait for the opponent to begin their swing animation. Press **F** just before the hit lands to trigger a parry, stunning them briefly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Execute the Core Combo">
    The most effective basic combo is **M1 -> M1 -> Critical**. Using the Critical hit as the third strike often breaks the opponent's guard if they try to block.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Utilize Mobility">
    Use the **Q key** to dash. Dashing can be used to close the gap or to retreat when your stamina is low. Never stay stationary for too long.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Spacing is the final piece of the puzzle. You must "feather" your M1s, meaning you should put slight delays between your clicks. This makes your attack rhythm unpredictable and much harder to parry. If you find yourself being parried constantly, you are likely clicking too fast and becoming predictable.

| Action | Keybind | Tactical Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Light Attack** | M1 (Left Click) | Chip damage and combo building |
| **Critical Hit** | Heavy Attack Key | Breaking guards and finishers |
| **Parry / Block** | F Key | Defensive timing and counter-initiation |
| **Dash** | Q Key | Evading attacks or closing distance |
| **Sprint** | Shift Key | Repositioning during neutral play |

<Callout type="warning" title="Stamina Management">
Do not deplete your stamina bar completely. If you run out of stamina while being attacked, you cannot dash or parry, effectively making you a "sitting duck" for the opponent's full combo.
</Callout>

</Section>
