<Section icon={Shield} title="Countering Legendary Styles">

Legendary styles represent the pinnacle of the combat meta. They feature powerful passive abilities and highly dangerous critical attacks. Fighting them requires patience and precise timing.

### Boxing
The Boxing style is notorious for its speed. Its first M1 attack has a hitbox that actually registers slightly before the physical animation plays out. To deal with the Boxing critical, keep in mind that it consists of two distinct hits. If you maintain full posture, you can safely block both hits. If the first hit connects, immediately attempt to parry the second.

### Wrestling
Wrestling features the most devastating critical strike in the game, backed by robust hyper-armor. Passive play is highly effective here; smart Wrestlers will bait you into attacking so they can trade with their critical. If you get slammed, your screen becomes severely blurred for three to four seconds. During this window, you cannot dash, or you will trip and fall. 

### Kensei (Curie)
The Kensei style utilizes a unique debuff that prevents you from parrying for two seconds after its critical lands. Do not panic and try to parry during this lockout. Instead, immediately hold your block to absorb two M1 strikes, then dash away to reset the neutral game.

### Capoeira
Capoeira players benefit from a passive 25% reduction in dash cooldowns. Expect them to play highly mobile, moving in and out of your range constantly. While their M1 animations are erratic, they become highly predictable once you familiarize yourself with their rhythm.

<Section icon={Target} title="Beating Rare and Common Styles">

Rare and Common styles are significantly easier to handle due to their highly telegraphic animations and lack of hyper-armor. However, they can still punish unprepared players.

Karate and Muay Thai both rely heavily on ragdoll criticals. Unlike Wrestling, these criticals do not feature hyper-armor, meaning you can easily cancel them with well-timed M1 strikes or a quick dash. Karate players also benefit from passive posture regeneration, making passive defensive play against them less effective.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Range Assessment">
    Identify your opponent's height. If you are taller, abuse your reach to keep them outside of their critical range.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Crit Interruption">
    Watch for the startup animation of Karate or Muay Thai crits. Execute a quick M1 string to cancel the attack entirely.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Traditional Dash">
    If you miss the interruption window, execute a standard dash backward to completely avoid the ragdoll effect.
  </Step>
</Steps>

For Hikari and Slugger, the counter-play is incredibly simple. Both styles feature extremely slow, highly telegraphed M1 and critical animations. Simply watch their startup frames and parry every single incoming strike with ease.
