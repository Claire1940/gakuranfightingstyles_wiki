<Section icon={Shield} title="Advanced Combat Tactics">

To excel in **gakuran combat**, you must move beyond simple button mashing. The difference between a novice and a master is the ability to parry and manage spacing. Spacing is the art of staying just out of your opponent's reach while keeping them within yours.

**The Parry System**
Parrying is mapped to the **F** key. To successfully parry, you must press the key just before an opponent's strike lands. A successful parry leaves the attacker vulnerable for a split second, allowing you to start your own combo.

**Combo Breaking and Mix-ups**
Standard M1 combos are easy to predict. Most players will try to parry the third hit in a sequence. To counter this, use the "Two-Hit Critical" method:
1. Land two M1 strikes.
2. Immediately trigger your **Critical Hit** instead of the third M1.
3. This timing change often catches blockers off-guard, resulting in a guard break.

<Callout type="info" title="Movement Mechanics">
Use the **Q** key to dash. Dashing is essential for closing gaps against long-range Akari users or retreating when your stamina is low. Never exhaust your stamina completely, or you will be unable to block.
</Callout>

| Action | Keybind | Strategic Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Attack (M1)** | Left Click | Primary damage source; space them out |
| **Block / Parry** | **F** | Timing is key; don't just hold it |
| **Dash** | **Q** | Use for gap closing or dodging |
| **Critical Hit** | Varies | Use to break guards and end combos |
| **Toggle Combat** | **T** | Keep off unless you are ready to fight |

</Section>
