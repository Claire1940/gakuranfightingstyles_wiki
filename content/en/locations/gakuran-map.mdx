Beyond raw physical dimensions, there are two critical hidden stats that players must understand: Guard Pierce and Resilience. Guard Pierce determines the amount of chip damage your attacks deal to an opponent who is actively blocking. Resilience dictates your statistical probability of breaking out of a grapple, which occurs when both you and your opponent land a basic attack at the exact same moment.

<Callout type="info" title="Hidden Stats Strategy">

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Controls: Offense, Defense & Perfect Blocks">

Combat on the school grounds is fast-paced and highly competitive. To survive encounters across the campus, you must master the core control scheme. The system distinguishes between normal light attacks, heavy guard-breaking strikes, and defensive maneuvers.

| Keybind | Action | Attack Type | Tactical Description |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| T | Toggle Stance | Utility | Must be pressed to enable all offensive capabilities |
| Mouse 1 (M1) | Light Attack | Offensive | Can be chained up to four times; ends with a knockback kick |
| R | Heavy Attack | Offensive | High damage, unblockable, slow wind-up, ragdolls targets |
| F | Block | Defensive | Mitigates damage but rapidly consumes your posture bar |
| Q | Evasive Dodge | Defensive | Provides active invincibility frames (I-frames) |
| Ctrl | Interaction | Utility | Opens menus, activates photo modes, and starts mini-games |

Executing a successful offensive strategy requires balancing your light combos with heavy attacks. While the fourth hit of an M1 combo provides a valuable knockback that creates space, heavy attacks are perfect for punishing opponents who rely too heavily on standard blocking. However, because heavy attacks have a slow wind-up, they are easily parried by experienced players.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Activate Stance">
    Press the **T** key immediately upon encountering an enemy to enter your fighting stance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Engage with Light Combos">
    Execute up to three light attacks using **M1** to pressure your opponent's defense.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Bait and Perfect Block">
    Wait for the opponent to counter-attack, then press **F** exactly 0.2 seconds before the strike lands.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Punish with Heavy Strike">
    While the opponent is stunned from your perfect block, press **R** to execute an unblockable heavy slam.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Defending yourself requires careful monitoring of your posture bar. Holding down the block key indefinitely will result in a guard break once the bar empties, leaving your character stunned and completely vulnerable for two full seconds.

<Callout type="warning" title="Guard Break Danger">
A two-second stun is more than enough time for an opponent to execute an entire high-damage combo. Avoid holding block continuously; instead, use evasive dodges to reposition into your opponent's blind spots.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Meta Fighting Styles: Boxing vs. Wrestling">

The current meta features eight unique fighting styles, but two dominate the competitive landscape depending on your character's height tier. Choosing the correct style for your build is the difference between struggling in skirmishes and dominating the school courtyard.

| Fighting Style | Best Suited Height | Core Strengths | Signature Advantage |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| Boxing | Short to Medium | Incredible agility, rapid strikes, uncounterable heavies | Perfect blocks reduce heavy cooldowns to 1 second |
| Wrestling | Tall | Uninterruptible grabs, high crowd control, maximum defense | Heavy attack slams cannot be canceled by incoming strikes |

For shorter, highly mobile characters, Boxing is the undisputed king. It allows for an aggressive, fast-paced playstyle where you can constantly pressure opponents with rapid-fire punches. The key highlight of this style is its synergy with defense: performing a perfect block instantly slashes your heavy attack cooldown to just one second, allowing you to launch relentless counter-offensives.

Wrestling is the ultimate choice for tall, high-health characters. Because tall builds naturally possess a massive reach, Wrestling allows you to easily grab and slam opponents into the ground. These grab animations are entirely uninterruptible, meaning you can initiate a slam even while taking light damage. Additionally, Wrestling offers the highest base Resilience stat in the game, ensuring you almost always win grapple struggles.

<Callout type="tip" title="Style Selection Rule">
Do not force a Wrestling style on a short character. The lack of reach will make it incredibly difficult to land your uninterruptible heavy grabs, rendering the style's main advantage useless.
</Callout>
