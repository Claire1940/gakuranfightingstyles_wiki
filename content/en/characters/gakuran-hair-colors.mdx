While hair color is a personal choice, your nameplate color is a public declaration of your hard work and time spent in the game. Many players confuse **gakuran hair colors** with the colored outlines above their heads. These outlines are not cosmetic; they are tied directly to your school year.

The game automatically updates these colors as you progress. You do not need to visit an NPC or spend currency to change your nameplate from blue to purple. It serves as a visual rank system that lets everyone in the server know exactly how much experience you have accumulated.

| Nameplate Color | Academic Year | Social Status |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blue Outline** | First Year | New Student / Freshman |
| **Purple Outline** | Second Year | Experienced / Sophomore |
| **Yellow Outline** | Third Year | Senior / Master Student |

<Callout type="warning" title="Progression Warning">
You cannot manually equip a yellow nameplate just because you like the color. If you see a player with a yellow outline, they have legitimately reached the third year through the school system.

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Change Your Nameplate and Hair">

If you are looking to change your appearance or move up the ranks, you need to follow specific paths. While **gakuran hair colors** are changed through the UI, your nameplate requires gameplay effort.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Access Customization">
    Open the main menu and locate the 'Character' or 'Customization' tab. This is where you can select your base hair style and primary hair color.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Your Surname">
    Choose a surname that reflects your character's identity. The game will automatically convert this into Japanese Kanji or Katakana on your nameplate.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Attend Classes">
    To change your nameplate color, you must attend scheduled classes and complete academic tasks. This is the only way to earn the XP required for promotion.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Reach Year Milestones">
    Once your academic XP hits the threshold for Year 2, your nameplate outline will instantly turn purple. Continue this process to reach the yellow Year 3 status.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Kanji vs Katakana">
If your chosen surname has a traditional Japanese Kanji form, the game will prioritize displaying it in Kanji. If no Kanji exists for that name, it will use Katakana, which is the phonetic version used for foreign names.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Advanced Customization and Name Logic">

Understanding how your name is displayed is just as important as choosing your **gakuran hair colors**. The nameplate displays your character's surname (last name) rather than their first name, maintaining the traditional Japanese school atmosphere.

| Display Type | Logic | Visual Style |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Kanji** | Used for names with official Japanese characters | Complex, traditional strokes |
| **Katakana** | Used for phonetic translations of foreign names | Sharp, simplified characters |
| **Outline** | Determined strictly by school year | Glowing border around the name |

The combination of a unique hair color and a high-tier nameplate creates a distinct presence in the game. Most high-level players spend significant time balancing their aesthetic "drip" with their academic rank to ensure they look the part of a senior student.
