<Section icon={Target} title="Offensive Maneuvers and Combo Execution">

The offensive aspect of **gakuran controls** revolves around the synergy between light and heavy strikes. Mastery of the M1 combo is essential for maintaining pressure on your opponent. A standard M1 sequence consists of four consecutive hits. The final hit in this sequence is a specialized kick that provides a significant knockback effect. This knockback is crucial because it creates a window for follow-up attacks or allows you to reset your positioning if your health is low.

Heavy attacks, mapped to the **R** key, are the primary way to deal with players who rely heavily on blocking. While these attacks deal nearly double the damage of a standard M1, they are significantly slower. This slow wind-up makes them vulnerable to parries. Therefore, heavy attacks should be used strategically—typically when an opponent is cornered or right after a successful dodge.

| Attack Type | Damage Output | Knockback Power | Blockability |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **M1 Combo (1-3)** | Low-Medium | Minimal | Blockable |
| **M1 Finisher (4th)** | Medium | High (Kick) | Blockable |
| **Heavy Attack (R)** | Very High | Extreme (Ragdoll) | Unblockable |
| **Panic Attack** | Variable | Low | Spammable |

In addition to standard strikes, players can execute a "Panic Attack" by rapidly spamming M1 during high-pressure situations. While less technical, it can sometimes disrupt an opponent's rhythm. However, relying on this often leaves you open to more skilled players who understand the timing of perfect blocks.

<Callout type="warning" title="Heavy Attack Risks">
Because the **R** key heavy attack is slow, experienced players will often wait for the animation to start and then immediately attempt a perfect block. Use heavy attacks sparingly against high-skill opponents.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Defensive Systems and Perfect Blocking">

Defense is arguably more important than offense in the current meta of **gakuran controls**. The game utilizes a **Posture Bar** system, which determines how many hits you can absorb while blocking. If you hold the **F** key continuously, your posture bar will drain rapidly. Once empty, you suffer a "Guard Break," resulting in a 2-second stun that leaves you completely defenseless.

To circumvent posture drain, you must master the **Perfect Block**. This mechanic occurs when you press the **F** key exactly 0.2 seconds before an attack lands. Success in perfect blocking results in zero posture loss and briefly stuns the attacker for 0.5 seconds, giving you a massive advantage.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Observe Attack Patterns">
    Watch your opponent's animation wind-up to predict whether they are using a light M1 or a heavy R attack.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Time the Input">
    Press the **F** key just as the strike is about to connect. This requires a low ping for maximum consistency.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Counter-Attack">
    Immediately follow a perfect block with an M1 combo or a style-specific move to capitalize on the opponent's brief stun.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Reset Posture">
    If your posture bar is low, use the **Q** key to dodge away and allow the bar to regenerate naturally.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The dodge mechanic (**Q**) is equally vital. It provides "I-frames" (invincibility frames), allowing you to pass through attacks without taking damage. This is the most effective way to deal with heavy attacks that cannot be blocked normally. By dodging to the side, you can reach an opponent's "blind spot," opening them up for a back-attack.

| Defensive Move | Resource Cost | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Block** | High Posture | Safe damage reduction |
| **Perfect Block** | None | Stuns enemy and negates all damage |
| **Dodge (Q)** | Stamina/Cooldown | Provides I-frames and repositioning |
| **Healing (Clinic)** | Time | 3x faster health regeneration |

<Callout type="success" title="Perfect Block Reward">
If you are using the Boxing style, a successful perfect block reduces your heavy attack cooldown to just 1 second, allowing for aggressive counter-play.
</Callout>

Resilience increases your chances of winning a "Grapple," which occurs when two players land an M1 at the exact same time. Higher resilience allows you to break out of these grapples faster and counter-attack.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Advanced Fighting Styles and Meta Picks">

Once you have mastered the basic **gakuran controls**, you must choose a fighting style to specialize your combat capabilities. There are currently eight unique styles in the game, but two stand out as the "meta" choices for 2026.

**Boxing Style**
Boxing is the premier choice for short and mid-sized players. It focuses on extreme aggression and I-frame utilization. The heavy attacks in this style are notoriously difficult to counter, often forcing opponents to waste their dodge cooldown. The reduction of heavy attack cooldowns upon a perfect block makes the Boxer a relentless force on the battlefield.

**Wrestling Style**
Wrestling is the dominant choice for tall players. Its heavy attack involves grabbing the opponent and slamming them into the ground—a move that cannot be interrupted once initiated. Furthermore, Wrestling provides the highest resilience stat in the game, making it nearly impossible to lose a grapple fight against a non-wrestler.
