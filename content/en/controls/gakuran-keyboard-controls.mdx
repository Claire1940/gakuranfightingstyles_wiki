<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics and Offensive Inputs">

The combat system in Gakuran is visceral and rewards players who can chain their inputs effectively. Unlike simple clicker games, Gakuran requires a mix of light and heavy attacks to bypass the various defensive layers of your opponents. Understanding the frame data—or at least the "feel"—of your attacks is vital for high-level play in 2026.

| Attack Type | Input | Speed | Damage | Strategic Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Basic Attack** | Left Click | Fast | Moderate | Main damage source, used for standard combos |
| **Heavy Attack** | **R** | Slow | High | Breaking blocks and disrupting enemy timing |
| **Style Skill** | **T** | Variable | Massive | Finishing combos or applying status effects |
| **Execution** | **B** | Static | Lethal | Finishing off downed players to prevent recovery |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initiate with Light Attacks">
    Start your engagement with a series of Left Clicks. These are fast and hard to react to, allowing you to "check" your opponent's defense.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Mix in Heavy Attacks">
    If your opponent is holding their block, press **R**. The heavy attack is designed to punish passive play and can often lead to a guard break.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Deploy Fighting Style Skills">
    Once you have successfully landed a few hits, use **T** to activate your specific fighting style skill. These usually have longer cooldowns but deal significant burst damage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Combat Warning">
Do not spam the **T** skill randomly. Because these skills often have long animations, missing one leaves you wide open to a counter-attack that could cost you the entire match.
